# G6 — Graceful degradation (yolo_onnx kill mid-goal)

**Date:** 2026-04-19
**Purpose:** Confirm Nav2 survives a mid-mission failure of `yolo_onnx_node`
(the monocular YOLO front-end). Measure `/vision_obstacles` collapse after the
kill (should drop to 0 Hz) and `/plan` continuity (should remain roughly
flat — Nav2 is LiDAR-only for obstacles, §4.15 scope).

## Setup

- Pi stack: `robot.launch.py` (HAL, no perception) + `lidar.launch.py` + at
  t=5 s `slam_localization.launch.py map:=room1.yaml` + at t=12 s
  `navigation.launch.py` + at t=22 s `perception_pi.launch.py`. Script:
  `report/scripts/g6_pi_bringup.sh` (composed outside `explore.launch.py`
  because that launch hard-codes mapping mode).
- Dev PC: `start_monitoring.sh --mode navigation --no-record` for RViz 2D
  Pose Estimate and visual confirmation of goals.
- Room1 map used (saved Phase 2 occupancy grid).
- AMCL initial pose set via RViz at the robot's actual starting location
  before trials.
- Battery at bringup: 8.05 V (2S, freshly charged, well above 7.2 V sag
  threshold).
- Test-area constraint: ~0.5 m free on either side of the robot, so the
  goal distance was trimmed from the planned 1.5 m to 0.3 m; the kill
  timing was brought in from t=5 s to t=3 s to fire during active
  planning rather than after goal completion.

## Procedure (per trial)

1. `g6_graceful_degradation.py --trial N` on Dev PC.
2. Script reads TF `map → base_footprint` for home pose, (re)launches
   perception via `/home/ubuntu/shared/g6_yolo_restart.sh` on the Pi if
   YOLO isn't already up, waits 25 s for ONNX model load, then publishes
   a 0.3 m forward (odd trials) or backward (even trials) goal on
   `/goal_pose`.
3. At t+3 s the script SSHes to the Pi and
   `pkill -9 -f yolo_onnx_node`.
4. Subscribers log every `/vision_obstacles` and `/plan` arrival for 20 s
   post-goal. Summary row goes to
   `report/data/graceful_degradation.csv`; per-trial event timestamps go
   to `graceful_degradation_trial{N}_events.csv` for the plot script.
5. Trial direction alternates (`+x, -x, +x`) so the robot oscillates
   around its starting pose within the 0.5 m corridor rather than
   drifting outside it.

## Observations

- **Graceful degradation verified.** `/vision_obstacles` fires at 1–3 Hz
  pre-kill (when a suitcase sits in the robot's FOV) and flat-lines to
  0 Hz within one scan period of the YOLO kill in every trial. No
  error, no node restart — the downstream `depth_estimator_node`
  simply runs out of upstream `/detections` to back-project, which is
  the intended failure mode.
- **Nav2 survives.** `/plan` holds ~0.9 Hz through the kill event and
  the robot reaches the goal (≤4 cm error) every trial. Nav2 never
  crashed, no `bt_navigator` SIGABRT, no recovery behaviours invoked.
- **Post-kill `/plan` rate decay is reach-related, not kill-related.**
  Once the robot is within goal tolerance, Nav2 stops emitting fresh
  plans — the drop from ~1 Hz to 0 Hz in the bottom panel of the
  figure happens at t≈8–10 s, well after the t=3.3 s kill.
- **"Robot stops on suitcase" is LiDAR, not vision.** The suitcase was
  picked up by the MS200 and marked on the LiDAR costmap; Nav2
  re-planned around it. `observation_sources` is still LiDAR-only per
  the 2026-04-15 sensor-scope decision, so this is orthogonal to the
  G6 metric.

## Problems hit + fixes

- `pgrep -f yolo_onnx_node` inside `bash -lc '...'` matched the wrapper's
  own argv (§3.2 Gotcha 2 from `bot_testing_instructions.md`), so the
  relaunch guard always thought YOLO was alive and skipped the
  relaunch. Fixed by dropping `-f` (comm match only).
- `ros2 run af_perception yolo_onnx_node` without the launch file's
  parameters brings up a bare node that can't find its ONNX model. Fixed
  by invoking `ros2 launch af_perception perception_pi.launch.py` via a
  Pi-side helper script (`shared/g6_yolo_restart.sh`) rather than
  heredoc'ing it through SSH.
- `g6_pi_bringup.sh` uses `slam_localization.launch.py` rather than
  piggy-backing on `start_pi.sh` / `explore.launch.py`, which both hard-
  code mapping mode. Kept the same staggered timeline (t=0 HAL+LiDAR,
  t=5 SLAM, t=12 Nav2, t=22 perception).

## Verdict

**PASS.** Graceful degradation and Nav2 survival are demonstrated across
all three trials. The critical observable — `/vision_obstacles` going
to 0 Hz with no downstream disruption — is consistent.

## Paragraph for §5.5

> I validated Nav2's resilience to a mid-mission perception failure by
> killing `yolo_onnx_node` three seconds into a short navigation goal
> and logging `/vision_obstacles` and `/plan` arrival times for the
> next 17 s. Across three trials (N=3, 0.3 m forward/backward goals,
> battery 8.05 V, room1 AMCL localisation), `/vision_obstacles`
> collapsed from 1–3 Hz to 0 Hz within one scan period of the kill in
> every run, while `/plan` continued publishing at ~0.9 Hz and the
> robot reached its goal with a mean 3.0 cm error (σ 0.9 cm). No
> `bt_navigator` crash, recovery behaviour, or costmap abort was
> observed. The result confirms the intended degradation path —
> `depth_estimator_node` silently runs out of upstream detections, the
> LiDAR-only obstacle layer keeps Nav2 fed, and the rest of the stack
> is unaffected.
